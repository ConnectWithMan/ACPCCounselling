ACPC Counselling FAQs

Got Questions? We've Got Answers.

Find answers to all your doubts regarding Gujarat degree engineering and pharmacy admissions, registration, choice filling, and more.

General Admission & Registration FAQs

What is ACPC and what is its role?

ACPC stands for the Admission Committee for Professional Courses. It is a committee formed by the Government of Gujarat to regulate and conduct centralized online counselling for admissions into professional degree courses like B.Tech, Pharmacy, MBA, MCA, and Architecture in the state.

Who is eligible to participate in ACPC counselling?

Candidates who have passed their Class 12 board examinations (Science stream) from a recognized board in Gujarat or hold a Gujarat domicile, and have appeared for the respective entrance exams (like GUJCET for B.Tech/Pharmacy or JEE Main) are generally eligible. Please check the specific eligibility criteria for each course.

Do I need to submit original documents physically?

During the initial registration phase, documents are verified online, so physical submission is not required. However, once you are allotted a seat and report to your admitted institute, you must produce original documents for final verification.

What is the Tuition Fee Waiver (TFW) scheme?

The TFW scheme allows economically weaker students (whose family income is less than Rs. 8 Lakhs per annum) to get a complete waiver on tuition fees. You must opt for 'Yes' in the TFW section during registration and upload a valid income certificate to be eligible.

Is there an upper age limit for applying through ACPC?

There is no specific upper age limit prescribed by ACPC for admission to undergraduate engineering (B.Tech) and pharmacy programs.

Can diploma holders apply for degree engineering through ACPC?

Yes, diploma holders can apply for degree engineering directly into the second year through the Diploma to Degree (D2D) counselling process conducted by ACPC.

Can I edit my application form after submission?

Generally, once the form is submitted and locked, you cannot edit it. However, ACPC provides a specific grievance/query window during which you can request corrections for errors in marks or categories.

Is Aadhaar card mandatory for ACPC registration?

Yes, providing your Aadhaar card number is highly recommended and typically mandatory for identity verification and scholarship processing.

Are students from NIOS board eligible?

Yes, students from the National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) are eligible provided their study center was located in Gujarat, or they fulfill the criteria under the All India Quota.

What is the Non-Creamy Layer (NCL) certificate requirement?

Candidates applying under the SEBC category must provide a valid Non-Creamy Layer (NCL) certificate issued by the Government of Gujarat (in Gujarati) as per the specified financial year.

Does ACPC conduct its own entrance exam?

No, ACPC does not conduct any entrance exams. It relies on exams like GUJCET, JEE Main, NATA, and CMAT conducted by other authorities for admissions.

What is the fee for ACPC registration?

The online registration fee is typically around Rs. 350 to Rs. 500 depending on the course. The exact amount is mentioned in the official admission brochure each year.

Can I register offline for ACPC counselling?

No, the entire ACPC registration, choice filling, and seat allotment process is conducted strictly online through their official portal.

What is the validity of the Income Certificate for TFW?

The income certificate must be issued on or after April 1st of the preceding financial year. In Gujarat, it is generally valid for 3 financial years, but you must check the latest brochure for exact dates.

Merit List & Cutoffs FAQs

How is the merit rank calculated for B.Tech?

For state-quota seats, merit is calculated giving 60% weightage to the percentile of Class 12 board theory marks (PCM) and 40% weightage to the GUJCET percentile.

What is a Provisional Merit List?

It is an initial, draft ranking of candidates based on the data they submitted. It allows candidates to check for discrepancies and raise queries before the final list is made.

What is a Final Merit List?

The final merit list is published after resolving all queries and document discrepancies. This rank is binding and is used for all actual seat allotments.

Can two students have the same merit rank?

ACPC applies specific tie-breaking rules (like comparing Math marks, then Physics marks, then age, etc.) to ensure no two students hold the exact same merit rank.

Where can I find previous years' cutoffs?

Previous years' opening and closing ranks (cutoffs) are published on the official gujacpc.admissions.nic.in website and on our Cutoffs page.

What is the difference between Category Rank and State Rank?

State rank represents your standing among all applicants in Gujarat. Category rank represents your standing only among the applicants within your specific reservation category (e.g., SC, ST, SEBC).

If my board marks increase after re-evaluation, will my merit rank change?

Yes, if you submit the revised marksheet to ACPC during the grievance period before the final merit list is generated, your rank will be recalculated.

Choice Filling & Seat Allotment FAQs

What is a Mock Round in ACPC?

The Mock Round is a trial or practice round of choice filling. It helps candidates understand the software, gauge the cutoffs, and see which college or branch they might be allotted based on their merit rank. The allotment in this round is not actual or binding.

Can I change my choices after the Mock Round?

Yes, after the Mock Round results are declared, candidates are given a window to add, remove, rearrange, or delete choices before the final locking for Round 1 of actual seat allotment.

How many choices can I fill during ACPC choice filling?

There is no limit. You can fill as many choices (combinations of college and branch) as you want from the available options.

What should be the order of my choices?

You should arrange your choices strictly in the decreasing order of your actual preference, placing your absolute dream college/branch at the top, regardless of your rank.

Does locking choices early give me an advantage?

No. Seat allotment is done purely on the basis of merit rank. Locking choices on the first day or the last day has no impact on your chances.

What happens if I forget to lock my choices?

If you forget to manually lock your choices, the system will automatically lock your last saved choices when the deadline expires.

Can I participate in Round 2 if I didn't register initially?

Yes, ACPC typically opens a fresh registration window for non-registered candidates before Round 2 begins, allowing them to participate in vacant seats.

What is Reshuffling or Upgradation Round?

Round 2 is often called the reshuffling round. It allows you to try for better choices. If you don't get an upgraded seat, your Round 1 seat remains secure.

If I get a better seat in Round 2, what happens to my Round 1 seat?

If a new seat is allotted in Round 2, your Round 1 seat is automatically cancelled and allotted to the next eligible candidate. You cannot claim it back.

Can I retain my Round 1 seat if I don't get any new seat in Round 2?

Yes, if no new seat is allotted to you based on your higher preferences in Round 2, your Round 1 seat remains perfectly safe.

Token Fee, Cancellation & Reporting FAQs

What happens if I don't pay the token fee after seat allotment?

If a seat is allotted to you in a round and you fail to pay the token tuition fee online within the stipulated deadline, your admission to that specific seat will be cancelled, and you may lose the seat.

How do I pay the token tuition fee?

The token tuition fee must be paid strictly online through the ACPC portal using Net Banking, Credit Card, Debit Card, or UPI.

Will my token fee be refunded if I cancel my admission?

Yes, if you cancel your admission online within the allowed timeframe, the token fee is refunded. The refund rules usually state that full refund is given if your cancelled seat is claimed by another student in subsequent rounds.

Can I cancel my admission online?

Yes, ACPC provides an online admission cancellation module on your dashboard. You do not need to visit the admission committee physically.

What is the Management Quota (MQ)?

Private/Self-Financed institutes reserve a certain percentage (usually up to 50%) of seats known as the Management Quota. These seats are filled directly by the institutes based on merit, not through the centralized ACPC software.

What happens to vacant seats after all ACPC rounds?

After all centralized rounds are over, any remaining vacant seats in Government and Private institutes are handed over to the respective institutes. They conduct their own offline/institute-level rounds to fill them on merit.
